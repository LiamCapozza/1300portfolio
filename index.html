Case Studies

Iterative Design

Intro

This project was a collaboration with fellow Brown students to work with a startup called Mi Fonda. They are a restaurant banking system that allows for streamlined income and expenses.


They tasked us with creating an app for their clients, which would be a complete overhaul when compared to their website.


Throughout the project we had lots of back and forth emails with the CTO of Mi Fonda. This was a valuable experience with corporate communication and adapting to the needs of a userbase

Starting Out

This is the Fonda current website and they do not have an app. Our project group was tasked with creating an app to display the information on the go, with a new, more modern look

Sketching My Ideas

My first flow incorporated more elements from their original site while my second flow felt more like an app.

During my sketches, it became apparent to me that the second flow felt right and was better for seeing and understanding information quickly and on the go. It applied more mobile practices and was the flow I ultimately pulled the most from throughout the project.

Collaborating and Wireframing

We had a basic login screen that was up to par with one’s found on many types of apps. We wanted to keep things simple and modern. The dashboard is supposed to be an “at a glance” view of the account. It displays the balance as well as a donut graph to show different expenses.

We knew we wanted to display both the revenue and expenses and decided later on to use a bar graph. Finally, our account info screen had space for us to add multiple different buttons in three categories, Personal, Security, and Other.

Final Product

Throughout the design we used the Roboto font which aligned with the style guide given to us by Fonda. We utilized the different heading and body text sizes to emphasize more important points, such as the balance on the dashboard or the day of the week in transactions.

We also made sure to change our design to emphasize contrast of colors, since part of our feedback was changing the transactions to have a light background instead of dark.

Conclusion

Working on this app with Mi Fonda was a great first experience in startup companies. We got to improve our design skills and learn how to adjust our work to meet real business needs.

It was very important to adapt and improve, which was challenging but rewarding. This project was a solid crash course in navigating client needs and delivering a product that makes a difference.

Personas

Intro

This case study focuses on selecting a physical interface in the real world and creating personas based upon research and interviews conducted. I chose the Coca Cola FreeStyle machine that can be found in restaurants all across the country.

Chosen Interface

I decided on the freestyle machine due to its abudnace on campus here and since everyone uses it when visiting the dining hall. Its relative simplicity made it a good target for user research.

FreeStyle Machine Breakdown

The Freestyle machine has a single spout that can dispense hundreds of drink options.

Choose a base drink and add any flavor, press the dispense button, and a custom drink pours out from the spout.

Interviewing Students

I wanted to see what students had to say about this machine. Each of the following were observed during their use and approached afterwards to answer some questions. These were the takeaways from each discussion.

Person 1

Observations: Approached the machine quickly. Had to hit the 'back' button multiple times. Chose Barq’s Root Beer Zero with no flavors. Made decisions quickly and used the ice dispenser.

Interview Insights: Found the process very clear and user-friendly, even for first-timers. Uses the machine regularly, likes the organized flavor options, never used the QR code, enjoys seasonal drinks.

Summary: Likes a quick and efficient interaction, with minor issues on responsiveness.

Person 2

Observations: Waited in line, chose Powerade with lemon-lime flavor quickly, had unresponsive 'back' button issues.

Interview Insights: Machine usage is very easy, frequent use after practice for their sport, focused mainly on Powerade, didn't know about the QR code feature.

Summary: Mainly uses the machine for hydration needs, wants quick access and options over other features.

Person 3

Observations: Took time deciding, chose Coke with vanilla, had issues with 'back' button.

Interview Insights: Finds the machine intuitive and easy, uses occasionally, appreciates the well-organized and informative screens, didn't know about the QR code.

Summary: Enjoys creating custom drinks leisurely, likes the machine's variety and ease of use but faces slight frustrations with screen responsiveness.

Personas

Healthy Hailey

Healthy Hailey is a college athlete who wants to get a quick drink either before or after practice. If before, she wants a caffeinated drink with no calories, and if after she wants an electrolyte drink that is hydrating.

Thinks "I don’t want too many cals in my drink. Maybe this has a healthier alternative. Will people judge me for this drink?"
Feels Tired from classes and practices, rushed because of limited free time, behind on work due to diverse focus.
Says "I wonder if there are any cals in this?", "Ah they don’t have my favorite flavor", "Sorry I can’t talk, I’m gonna be late to class."
Does Try to multitask by eating and doing work in the dining hall, double-checks to make sure she got the diet or zero cal version, tries to get a drink before class.

Custom Carl

Custom Carl is a college student who wants a fun and tasty drink to go along with his meal. They aren’t super worried about the calories or caffeine of a drink, just that it tastes good.

Thinks "I wonder if ___ will taste good?", "I haven’t tried that in a while, maybe I will next time", "I hope the person behind me doesn’t care that I’m taking a while."
Feels Curious about trying new things, excited when he sees new seasonal flavors, sad if the flavors he wants are out of stock.
Says "Guys, try this it’s so good", "Man, this is terrible, why did I make this don’t ever try ___", "Quick, let's go grab another drink before the dining hall closes."
Does Mixes new flavors, gets multiple different drinks per meal, shares with his friends.

The personas are similar, they are both college students having a meal, but their wants are different.

Hailey is someone on the go with no time to spare, while Carl is someone looking for a more relaxed meal experience.

Storyboards

After practice Hailey rushes to the dining hall to get something quick before she is late for a lab.

She quickly pushes the button to filter to just the low calorie drinks and picks a caffeinated drink

She then is able to quickly make a decision since all her options are laid out in front of her conveniently

She decides she does not need any flavorings

She gets her drink by using the one spout

As quick as she came she leaves in a hurry to get to where she needs to be on time

Conclusion

I got a clearer pciture of what users like and what could be better about the Freestyle machine as I made the personas of "Healthy Hailey" and "Custom Carl." This expereince in user research allows me to put an emphasis on the priorities of the user while I am designing products in the future.

Development

Intro

The goal of this Development project was to create a list of items similar to an online store or list maker with sorting, filtering, and an aggregator like a cart or favorites list.

I did a competitive analysis on three companies who all had features that I thought about either implementing or improving on in my own project.

I decided to create a Hike To-Do List Maker since it combines my large passion of the outdoors with the objectives of the project.

Competitive Analysis

I chose Spotify, Kuhl, and All Trails to do my competitive analysis on.

AllTrails has a helpful map and extensive filtering options, but it doesn't have a sorting trails by key attributes like length or difficulty.

Kuhl's interface is visually appealing and user-friendly, but it doesn't have essential functionalities like filtering and sorting in its shopping cart.

Spotify does a good job in sorting and custom playlist options but doesn't have a filtering system for song selection.

Feature AllTrails Kuhl Spotify
Filter list/cart Yes No No
Sort list/cart No No Yes
Ability to create multiple lists/carts Yes No Yes
Easy add/unadd from list/cart Yes Yes No
Clear entire list/cart No Yes No
Graphic/In-depth visual representation Yes No Yes

Development Process

I decided to design an interface that prioritizes detailed hike information over maps, featuring a to-do list sidebar and central display for managing hikes with sorting, filtering, and list editing capabilities.

I implemented filtering and sorting on the hike cards, allowing users to sort by height and filter for hikes in New Hampshire rated as Hard.

Adding a hike to the to-do list removes it from the main area, replacing the add button with a remove button, to avoid duplicates.

Link to site

Additional features include a search bar, a collapsible to-do list, and other responsive elements to enhance design and development skills.

The to-do list displays items in the order they were added.

Conclusion

It was really cool to combine my love for the outdoors with technical design. My Hike To-Do List Maker brings together some of the features from AllTrails, Kuhl, and Spotify into one along with some features of my own.

This project not only enhanced my development skills but also allowed me to look at development from a user-based design approach, which previously I had not done. I will continue to design software with the user first in the future since I found it ended with a more solid and meaningful final product.
